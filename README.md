# hi, I'm Pragya 👋

I live in Dubai. I don't have a software background.
I just really like building things for people I care about.

---

It probably started in 5th grade when I accidentally became
my school's sudoku champion and begged my parents to let me
take abacus lessons. Something about that kind of problem —
the kind with a clear logic, a satisfying solution,
a feeling of *click* when it works — lit up my brain
in a way I didn't have words for yet.

I didn't have the background or the self-awareness then
to know: maybe I was always supposed to be a builder.

---

In 2021, I wasn't sure I was creative. So I decided to
find out. I made 100 things — a robot that wheeled around
by itself, clay sculptures, food I'd never cooked before,
a cover of a song, an apple pie from scratch.
All of it documented publicly on Twitter,
all of it terrifying, all of it mine.

By the end I believed I could figure out anything
if I was stubborn enough.

→ [100 things I made](https://x.com/pragueyerrr/status/1349722802002284547)

---

Then in 2022 I found a Python library called
[prettymaps](https://github.com/marceloprates/prettymaps)
that generates beautiful illustrated maps from real city data.
I had no idea what I was doing. I spent 7 hours across
2 days completely, stubbornly, embarrassingly determined
to make it work. I documented all of it publicly.

Tech people showed up in my replies and said:
*"this is just what software engineering is."*
Someone offered me a Junior Developer position
if I ever found myself in the US.
I sold a few maps for $75–100 each.

I didn't take the job. But I didn't forget what it felt
like to be that stubborn and have it work.
Those neurons — the puzzle ones, the abacus ones,
the sudoku ones — they were firing the whole time.

→ [that thread](https://x.com/pragueyerrr/status/1482407871069528073)

## what's here

**Four job boards, one for each friend.**

Aman is a creative. Sanobar is in marketing.
Nandita (NanBan) is a PM. Ankit makes videos.
They're all job hunting in Dubai, and Dubai's job market
is scattered across a dozen sites with no great way
to track anything.

So I built each of them a personal board — it scrapes
relevant roles every few hours, lets them score jobs
against their CV, and generates tailored resumes and
cover letters with one click. Each board is tuned to
their field, their colors, their name in the header.

It started as one project for one friend and became four.
That felt right.

→ [Aman's Board](https://github.com/pragueyerrr/aman-board) · [NanBan's Board](https://github.com/pragueyerrr/nanbans-board) · [Ankit's Board](https://github.com/pragueyerrr/ankits-board) · [Sanobar's Board](https://github.com/pragueyerrr/sanobars-board)

**Threadthulhu**

Visakan Veerasamy ([@visakanv](https://twitter.com/visakanv))
has been thinking in public on Twitter for 15 years.
206,000 tweets.

[Venkatesh Rao once called it a "threadthulhu"](https://venkateshrao.com/twitter-book/chapters/preface.html) —
a Lovecraftian hyperobject of ideas too tangled,
too densely interlinked, too promiscuously forking
to ever be fully tamed. He said it would take AGI
to properly render it.

I'm building it a map.

The geography is real: each tweet gets embedded into a vector,
dimensionality-reduced into 2D space via UMAP,
and clustered into neighborhoods via HDBSCAN.
The shape of the map *is* the shape of his thinking.
You explore it like a city — fog of war and all —
threads revealing themselves as you walk through them.

It's not done. Maps are honest about incompleteness.

→ [Threadthulhu](https://github.com/pragueyerrr/threadthulhu)
*(in progress)*
